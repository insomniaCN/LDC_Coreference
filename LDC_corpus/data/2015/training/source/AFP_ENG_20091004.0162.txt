<HEADLINE>
Football: One dead after Croat and Muslim fans clash
</HEADLINE>
<DATELINE>
SARAJEVO, Oct 4, 2009 (AFP)
</DATELINE>
<TEXT>
<P>
One person died from injuries after Croat and Muslim fans clashed in the
southern town of Siroki Brijeg ahead of its Bosnian Premier League match against
Sarajevo, police said.
</P>
<P>
"It seems that one person has died," police spokesman Damir Cutura told AFP.
</P>
<P>
National radio BH Radio 1 reported that a Sarajevo fan died in the town's
medical facility after sustaining serious injuries.
</P>
<P>
It added that there were several others injured, while fans demolished shops in
the town's centre and set at least three vehicles ablaze.
</P>
<P>
The league match was subsequently cancelled because of the fatality, radio
reported.
</P>
<P>
Following the 1992-1995 war Bosnia remains ethnically divided and violence
during major football matches occasionally occurs here.
</P>
<P>
The war that claimed at least 100,000 lives pitted the country's Croats, Muslims
and Serbs against each other.
</P>
