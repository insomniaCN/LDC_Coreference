<HEADLINE> 
Kuwaiti emir pardons Filipino maid in murder case 
< / HEADLINE> 
<DATELINE> 
MANILA , Philippines 2009-06-11 07:27:32 UTC 
< / DATELINE> 
<TEXT> 
<P> 
The ruler of Kuwait has pardoned a Filipino maid who was convicted of murdering her employer 's 7 - year - old son , and she will soon be allowed to return home to her family , the Philippine the vice president said Thursday .
< / P> 
<P> 
A Kuwaiti court convicted May Vecina of slitting the throat of 7 - year - old Salem al - Otaibi with a kitchen knife in January 2007 . She was also found guilty of attempting to murder the boy 's 11 - year - old brother and 18 - year - old sister , who both survived .
< / P> 
<P> 
Her lawyer said she was " temporarily insane " because of the employer 's alleged mistreatment .
< / P> 
<P> 
Sheik Sabah Al Ahmed Al Sabah commuted Vecine 's death sentence to life in jail last year , and he has recently pardoned her , Vice President Noli de Castro said in a statement .
< / P> 
<P> 
The pardon was signed June 1 , said Ed Malaya , spokesman for the Foreign Affairs Department .
< / P> 
<P> 
" I thank the Filipino people for their prayers and we are very grateful to the Kuwaiti government and the Emir for his compassion , " de Castro said , adding that Vecine will soon be heading home to be reunited with her family .
< / P> 
<P> 
Vecina was sentenced to death in July 2007 , but last year the emir commuted her sentence to life in prison . The pardon means she is free to leave Kuwait , and the Philippine ambassador to Kuwait , Ricardo Endaya , said she was expected in Manila in the next few days .
< / P> 
<P> 
President Gloria Macapagal Arroyo traveled to Kuwait in 2007 to plea for the life of another maid on death row , whose murder conviction was also commuted to life in prison .
< / P> 
<P> 
More than 500,000 domestic helpers from Asian countries work in Kuwait , and often complain of nonpayment of salaries and physical abuse .
< / P> 
<P> 
Nearly 10 percent of 90 million Filipinos work abroad -- many as nurses , maids , engineers , construction workers and seamen . Last year , overseas Filipinos sent home $ 16.4 billion , or 10 percent of the country 's gross domestic product , fueling domestic consumption that is a lynchpin of the economy .
< / P>
