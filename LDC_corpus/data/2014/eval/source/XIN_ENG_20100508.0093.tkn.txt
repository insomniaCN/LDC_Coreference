<HEADLINE> 
2nd LD Writethru : Laura Chinchilla sworn in as president of Costa 
Rica 
< / HEADLINE> 
<DATELINE> 
SAN JOSE , May 8 ( Xinhua ) 
< / DATELINE> 
<TEXT> 
<P> 
Laura Chinchilla on Saturday was sworn in as president of Costa Rica on Saturday 
in a ceremony at La Sabana Metropolitan Park , San Jose , Costa Rica . She becomes 
the country 's first female president in history .
< / P> 
<P> 
At 11:39 a.m. local time ( 1739 GMT ) Chinchilla put on the presidential sash of 
Costa Rica and kissed it . Prior to this , two new vice presidents of Costa Rica 
had been sworn in as well .
< / P> 
<P> 
During her acceptance speech she promised to work with all of Costa Rica 's 
political spectrum and citizens , perhaps a reflection of the compromises she 
will be forced to make as leader of a divided legislature .
< / P> 
<P> 
" Leadership consists in crafting agreements , " she said . " Costa Rica is our 
shared home and no one should seek a monopoly on the truth , " she added .
< / P> 
<P> 
She also said she would raise the nation 's education budget . She elaborated on 
these goals during her speech .
< / P> 
<P> 
" We will work as a team for more peace in Costa Rica , with more police so that 
Costa Rica 's children are free to enjoy the fruits of liberty , " Chinchilla said .
" We will also work to make Costa Rica more competitive , but also to make sure 
that the achievements of some do not conflict with the rights of others . " 
< / P> 
<P> 
Presidents of Mexico , Colombia , Ecuador , Georgia , Panama , Guatemala , Honduras , 
El Salvador , Nicaragua and Spain 's Crown Prince Felipe attended the ceremony as 
honored guests . Li Bin , Director of National Population and Family Planning 
Commission of China , also attended the inauguration ceremony as a special envoy 
of the Chinese government .
< / P> 
<P> 
Laura Chinchilla , the candidate of ruling party , won Costa Rica 's presidential 
election on Feb . 7 , 2010 with 46.8 percent of votes . Her term runs from 2010 to 
2014 .
< / P> 
<P> 
Traffic was closed in the center of Costa Rica 's capital , San Jose , due to a 
demonstration of around 200 people . Raul Ramirez , the head of San Jose 's police , 
told Costa Rica 's Radio Monumental that the participants were trade union 
members and that they had committed to maintaining peace while they expressed 
their views .
< / P>
